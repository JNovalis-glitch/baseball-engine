## Construction Hierarchy

Lineup construction follows this order:

1. Spreadsheet
2. Participation grid
3. Pitching and catching plan
4. Field-area rotation
5. Player assignment grid
6. Validation
7. Position grid
8. Coach review
9. Print

Position assignments must not begin until the participation grid has been
created and verified.

## Workbook Source Data

The actual roster workbook contains:

- player name
- return/new status
- rank
- pitcher flag
- coaches-kid flag
- notes
- availability by game/date
- home played
- away played
- total played
- total available
- remaining available
- scheduled but no-showed

The workbook is the authoritative source for roster selection and season
tracking.

## Game Rules

- 6 innings
- 10 defensive positions
- 12 players per game
- 2 bench players per inning
- every player sits exactly once whenever practical
- no consecutive bench innings
- no player should sit twice before all players sit once
- no new player sits the first inning
- coach pitch innings: 1, 2, 5, 6
- kid pitch innings: 3, 4

## Position Rules

- only A players are eligible for 1B
- A players are primary pitchers
- A players are primary kid-pitch catchers
- strongest defenders generally play the left side
- best outfielder plays LCF
- A players anchor and support developing players
- kid-pitch innings are good opportunities for B/C infield reps

## Defensive Priorities

Coach pitch:

1. 1B
2. SS
3. P
4. LCF
5. LF
6. 3B
7. 2B
8. RCF
9. RF
10. C

Kid pitch:

1. P
2. C
3. all remaining positions are developmental

## Validation Requirements

Every final lineup must verify:

- exactly 10 fielders per inning
- exactly 2 bench players per inning
- all rostered players accounted for
- no duplicate players
- no missing players
- pitcher assigned correctly
- catcher assigned correctly
- each player appears exactly once per inning
- no player assigned to both field and bench
- bench assignments match the planned rotation
- no consecutive bench innings
- only approved players at 1B
- development opportunities are reasonably distributed
- strong players occupy priority defensive positions

## Optimization Boundary

Administrative validity comes before optimization. A valid lineup is always
better than an attractive but invalid lineup.
